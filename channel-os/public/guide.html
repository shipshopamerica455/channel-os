What is Channel OS?

Channel OS is a YouTube content operating system — a single dashboard that replaces scattered spreadsheets, notes, and tools with one organised system for three channels.

Genius Junkie

Canva & small business tutorials

  • Tutorial & template content
  • Lead magnet opportunities
  • Blog/Pinterest spin-offs

ShipShopTV

Authority documentary content

  • Power, loyalty, collapse
  • Long-form mini-docs
  • Cinematic packaging

UltraHealth

Evidence-based health content

  • Trust-building education
  • Evergreen topic clusters
  • SEO blog repurposing

The app has four sections:

First-time Setup

The only thing you need to configure before using the app is your Anthropic API key. This powers all AI features — idea generation, health summaries, content clusters, and marketing plans.

  1. Open channel-os-app.onrender.com

  2. Click ⚙️ Settings at the bottom-left of the sidebar. A panel slides open.

  3. Paste your Anthropic API key (sk-ant-...) into the field. Get one at console.anthropic.com.

  4. Click Save Key. The amber dot next to Settings turns off, confirming it's active.

  5. Select your active channel from the top of the sidebar.

⚠️

Important: The API key is stored only in your browser's localStorage for this session. If you clear browser data or use a different browser, you'll need to re-enter it. It is never sent to Render or any third-party server.

Shorts Pipeline — Step by Step

  1. Click a step card to expand it and see the full explanation.

  2. Click the command button on the right to copy it to clipboard.

  3. Paste and run the command in your terminal inside the remotion-shorts/ folder.

  4. Repeat for each step in order. The rendered MP4 lands in remotion-shorts/out/short.mp4.

Long-form Pipeline — Step by Step

  1. Research & Outline — Write a one-page outline: hook, 3–5 sections, CTA. Use your Idea Lab angle and cluster context.

  2. Script — Use the Claude script generator or write manually. Target ~1 word per second (1,500 words ≈ 10-minute video).

  3. Record / Film — Record voiceover or on-camera footage. Keep takes short and labeled by section.

  4. Edit — Cut in Premiere, DaVinci, or CapCut. Hook in first 30 seconds, no dead air, chapters if over 8 minutes.

  5. Thumbnail — Design in Canva. 3–5 words max, high contrast, consistent channel style.

  6. Upload & Optimize — Add SEO title, description with timestamps, tags, playlist. Pin a first comment.

Tab 1 — Overview

The main dashboard. Shows key metrics, channel health score, AI-generated summary, and your channel's built-in strategy rules.

Uploading YouTube Analytics

  1. Go to YouTube Studio → Analytics → Advanced Mode.

  2. Set your date range, select the metrics you want (Views, Watch time, CTR, Avg view duration), then click Export current view → CSV.

  3. Back in Channel OS, click 📤 Upload CSV in the top-right of Channel Intelligence.

  4. Select the exported file. The app parses it instantly and populates all four metric cards.

💡

The CSV parser handles YouTube Studio's standard export format automatically. It reads columns like Video title, Views, Watch time (hours), Impressions click-through rate (%), Average view duration.

Generating an AI Health Summary

  1. Upload your CSV first (optional but gives better results).

  2. Add any notes in the Notes & Signals tab (wins, failures, audience comments).

  3. Click ✨ Generate Summary. Claude analyses your data against your channel strategy.

  4. The summary appears showing: top topics, winning formats, winning hooks, growth recommendations, and warnings.

Tab 2 — Video Data

A full sortable table of all uploaded videos, ordered by views. Shows title, views, CTR (color-coded green/amber/red), average view duration, watch hours, and publish date.

Tab 3 — Notes & Signals

A manual notepad for capturing qualitative intelligence. Four note types:

🏆 Win

A video that overperformed. What worked? Hook, topic, format, thumbnail?

💥 Failure

A video that underperformed. What didn't land? Worth recording to avoid repeating.

👁 Observation

General patterns you're noticing — trends, algorithm behaviour, competitor moves.

💬 Audience Signal

Comments, DMs, or community responses that reveal what your audience wants next.

💡

Notes are fed directly into the AI when generating ideas and health summaries. The more specific your notes, the more targeted the AI output will be.

Generating Ideas with AI

  1. Make sure your API key is set in Settings.

  2. Click ✨ Generate Ideas in the top-right. Claude generates 8 ideas specifically for your active channel.

  3. Each idea is automatically scored for strategy fit and assigned a video type (Long-form or Short).

  4. Ideas appear in the Backlog tab instantly. Duplicates are filtered out automatically.

✅

For best results, upload analytics data and add channel notes in Channel Intelligence before generating ideas. Claude uses all of that context to make ideas more relevant and specific.

Tab 2 — Clusters & Series

  1. Click 🗂 Clusters in the top-right (requires API key).

  2. Claude analyses your existing backlog and channel pillars to generate 4–6 content clusters.

  3. Each cluster shows: cluster name, which pillar it belongs to, a description, and 3–5 specific video titles within it.

  4. Series-worthy clusters are flagged with a series name badge.

Tab 3 — Add Manually

Add ideas that came from your own thinking without AI:

  1. Select Long-form or ⚡ Short as the video type.

  2. Enter the title. A Strategy Fit Preview appears instantly, scoring the title against your channel's keywords and pillars.

  3. Optionally add a hook, format, and cluster/series name.

  4. Click Add to Backlog.

New Video End-to-End

Complete walkthrough from idea to published + promoted:

  1. Generate ideas in Idea Lab → filter for Strong Fit → pick the best one.

  2. Update its status to Planned on the idea card.

  3. Add it to the calendar in Marketing Planner → assign a week → select Long-form or Short.

  4. Go to Video Production → the idea appears in "Ready to Produce" → click Start → to move it to In Production.

  5. Follow the pipeline — copy each command and run it, or work through the manual checklist for Long-form.

  6. Upload to YouTube. Update the idea status to Published in Idea Lab.

  7. Work through the Promotion Checklist in Marketing Planner — tick off each platform as you post.

  8. Add a note in Channel Intelligence (Win or Observation) to record what performed well.

Tips & Tricks

Get better AI ideas

  • Upload fresh analytics CSV before generating ideas — Claude uses your top-performing video data to suggest similar winning topics.
  • Add audience signal notes (comments that reveal what people want) — these directly influence what Claude suggests.
  • Generate ideas multiple times — each run produces 8 new ideas, and duplicates are automatically filtered out.

Use clusters to plan series

  • Click 🗂 Clusters to see AI-generated series ideas. A series of 4–6 related videos performs significantly better than isolated one-offs.
  • Tag ideas with the same Cluster name to group them in the backlog view.

Shorts strategy

  • For Long-form videos: always check the Shorts Angle field on an idea card. Every long-form should have at least one Short cutdown.
  • Use the Shorts Selection Criteria in Marketing Planner → Repurposing to decide which moment to cut.
  • Tag Short ideas separately in the backlog so they don't get mixed with Long-form production planning.

Keep notes specific

  • Don't just write "video did well" — write why: "Hook mentioning specific tool name (Canva AI) got 8% CTR vs channel avg of 4%."
  • Paste in actual YouTube comments as Audience Signal notes — Claude reads these verbatim when generating ideas.

Flag off-brand ideas before they waste time

  • Any idea with an Off-Brand badge should be rejected immediately or reworked so it fits the channel's identity.
  • Use the Fit filter to show only Strong Fit ideas when you're in planning mode.
